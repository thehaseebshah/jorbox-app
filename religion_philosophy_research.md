# Religion & Philosophy Research for Lagao Tukey Trivia Game

## Overview
25 numerical estimation questions spanning various aspects of religion and philosophy, designed for a Wits and Wagers-style game where players estimate values they likely don't know exactly.

---

## Questions with Verified Answers

### 1. The Ten Commandments
**Question:** In what year (approximately) were the Ten Commandments given to Moses according to biblical chronology?
**Answer:** 1446 BCE
**Source:** Biblical chronology based on 1 Kings 6:1, which states the temple was built 480 years after the Exodus. Most scholars place this around 1446 BCE.
**Difficulty:** Medium
**Estimation Range:** 2000-1000 BCE

## Question Categories Distribution

### Population Statistics (8 questions)
- Major world religions (Christianity, Islam, Hinduism, Buddhism, Judaism, Sikhism, Baháʼí, Jainism)
- Vatican City population

### Historical Dates (7 questions)
- Founding figures and events
- Text compilations
- Religious origins

### Quantities/Counts (6 questions)

- Temples and shrines
- Religious texts structure

### Religious Concepts (4 questions)
- Number of Hindu deities
- Biblical and Quranic structure

## Difficulty Analysis

### Easy (4 questions)
- Large population numbers people might have heard
- Simple counts like Quran chapters

### Medium (12 questions)
- Specific population figures
- Historical dates within reasonable range
- Temple/shrine counts

### Hard (9 questions)
- Ancient historical dates
- Specific counts like saints and denominations
- Less common religious statistics

---

## Sources and Verification Methods

1. **Pew Research Center**: Primary source for religious demographics
2. **Vatican Records**: Official Catholic Church statistics
3. **Academic Sources**: Oxford Handbook, World Christian Encyclopedia
4. **Government Statistics**: National religious offices
5. **Historical Records**: Traditional religious chronologies

All figures represent the most recent available data as of 2023-2024, with cross-verification from multiple sources where possible.

---

## Game Design Notes

These questions work well for estimation games because:
- Most people have some context but don't know exact numbers
- Answers span different scales (hundreds to billions)
- Topics are generally familiar but specifics are obscure
- Range of difficulties allows for varied point opportunities
- Questions are clear and unambiguous
