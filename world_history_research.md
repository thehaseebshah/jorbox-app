# World History Questions for "Lagao Tukey" Trivia Game
*Research document with verified facts and sources*

## Questions About Years and Time Periods

### 1. In what year did the Roman Empire officially fall?
**Answer:** 476 CE
**Source:** Encyclopedia Britannica - The fall of the Western Roman Empire is traditionally dated to 476 CE when Romulus Augustulus was deposed.

### 2. How many years did the Hundred Years' War actually last?
**Answer:** 116 years (1337-1453)
**Source:** History.com - The Hundred Years' War was a series of conflicts between England and France that lasted from 1337 to 1453.

### 3. In what year did Columbus first reach the Americas?
**Answer:** 1492
**Source:** National Geographic - Christopher Columbus's first voyage reached the Americas on October 12, 1492.

### 4. How many years did the Ming Dynasty rule China?
**Answer:** 276 years (1368-1644)
**Source:** Britannica - The Ming Dynasty ruled China from 1368 to 1644.

### 5. In what year did World War I begin?
**Answer:** 1914
**Source:** History.com - World War I began in 1914 after the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand.

## Questions About Population and Demographics

### 6. What was the approximate world population in the year 1 CE?
**Answer:** About 200-300 million people
**Source:** UN Department of Economic and Social Affairs - Historical population estimates place world population at 200-300 million in 1 CE.

### 7. How many people died in the Black Death pandemic (1347-1351)?
**Answer:** About 75-200 million people
**Source:** CDC - The Black Death killed an estimated 75-200 million people in Eurasia.

### 8. What was the population of the Roman Empire at its peak around 117 CE?
**Answer:** About 60-80 million people
**Source:** Stanford University's ORBIS project - Roman Empire population estimates range from 60-80 million at its height.

### 9. How many people were killed during the Mongol Conquests (13th century)?
**Answer:** About 30-40 million people
**Source:** Historical Demography studies - Mongol conquests resulted in 30-40 million deaths across Eurasia.

### 10. What was the population of London during the Shakespearean era (around 1600)?
**Answer:** About 200,000 people
**Source:** British History Online - London's population was approximately 200,000 in 1600.

## Questions About Distances and Geography

### 11. How many miles long was the Great Wall of China at its peak?
**Answer:** About 13,000 miles (21,000 km)
**Source:** UNESCO World Heritage Centre - The Great Wall system included walls, trenches, and natural barriers totaling about 13,000 miles.

### 12. How many miles did the Silk Road span at its height?
**Answer:** About 4,000 miles (6,400 km)
**Source:** UNESCO - The Silk Road network spanned approximately 4,000 miles from China to the Mediterranean.

### 13. How deep were the trenches used in World War I?
**Answer:** About 7-10 feet deep (2-3 meters)
**Source:** Imperial War Museums - WWI trenches were typically 7-10 feet deep.

### 15. How many miles did the Berlin Wall stretch through the city?
**Answer:** About 96 miles (155 km)
**Source:** Berlin Wall Memorial - The Berlin Wall system totaled 96 miles including concrete walls and fortifications.

## Questions About Quantities and Numbers

### 16. How many soldiers fought in the Battle of Gettysburg?
**Answer:** About 165,000 soldiers total
**Source:** National Park Service - Approximately 165,000 soldiers fought at Gettysburg (93,000 Union, 72,000 Confederate).

### 17. How many pyramids were built in ancient Egypt?
**Answer:** About 118 pyramids identified
**Source:** Egyptian Ministry of Antiquities - Approximately 118 pyramids have been identified in Egypt.

### 18. How many books were in the Library of Alexandria at its peak?
**Answer:** About 400,000-700,000 scrolls
**Source:** Ancient sources including Plutarch - Estimates range from 400,000 to 700,000 scrolls (equivalent to 100,000-200,000 books).

### 19. How many people signed the U.S. Declaration of Independence?
**Answer:** 56 people
**Source:** National Archives - 56 delegates signed the Declaration of Independence.

### 20. How many years did it take to build the Great Pyramid of Giza?
**Answer:** About 20-27 years
**Source:** Archaeological studies - Based on workforce analysis and construction techniques.

## Questions About Costs and Economics

### 21. How much did the Louisiana Purchase cost in 1803 (in dollars)?
**Answer:** $15 million
**Source:** National Archives - The United States purchased Louisiana from France for $15 million.

### 22. How many pounds of tea were thrown into Boston Harbor during the Boston Tea Party?
**Answer:** About 342 chests (90,000 pounds)
**Source:** Boston Tea Party Ships & Museum - 342 chests of tea weighing approximately 90,000 pounds were destroyed.

### 23. What was the approximate cost of building the Panama Canal in today's dollars?
**Answer:** About $8-9 billion
**Source:** Panama Canal Authority - Adjusted for inflation, the canal cost approximately $8-9 billion in modern dollars.

### 24. How many slaves were transported from Africa to the Americas during the transatlantic slave trade?
**Answer:** About 12.5 million people
**Source:** UNESCO Slave Trade Database - Approximately 12.5 million Africans were transported to the Americas.

### 25. How many years did it take to build the Trans-Siberian Railway?
**Answer:** About 25 years (1891-1916)
**Source:** Russian State Railway Historical Museum - Construction spanned from 1891 to 1916.
