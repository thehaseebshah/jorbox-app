# Science & Nature Trivia Questions for "Lagao Tukey" Estimation Game

## Research Methodology
All questions have been verified through multiple reliable sources including NASA, scientific journals, government agencies, and educational institutions. Questions are designed to be estimable but not commonly known exact values.

---

## Physics & Astronomy

### 1. Speed of Light
**Question:** What is the speed of light in a vacuum in meters per second?
**Answer:** 299,792,458 m/s
**Source:** NASA, International Bureau of Weights and Measures
**Difficulty:** Medium
**Notes:** This is actually the definition of a meter - the distance light travels in 1/299,792,458 of a second.

### 2. Age of the Universe
**Question:** How old is the universe in billions of years?
**Answer:** 13.8 billion years
**Source:** NASA's WMAP and Planck satellite data
**Difficulty:** Medium
**Notes:** Based on cosmic microwave background radiation measurements.

### 3. Earth's Distance from Sun
**Question:** What is the average distance from Earth to the Sun in millions of kilometers?
**Answer:** 149.6 million km
**Source:** NASA, International Astronomical Union
**Difficulty:** Easy-Medium
**Notes:** This distance is defined as one Astronomical Unit (AU).

### 4. Temperature of Sun's Surface
**Question:** What is the surface temperature of the Sun in degrees Celsius?
**Answer:** 5,505°C
**Source:** NASA, Stanford Solar Center
**Difficulty:** Medium
**Notes:** This is the photosphere temperature; the corona is much hotter.

### 5. Number of Atoms in Human Body
**Question:** How many atoms are in an average human body (in octillions)?
**Answer:** 7 octillion atoms (7 × 10²⁷)
**Source:** Jefferson Lab, Physics Factbook
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Notes:** Based on average 70kg adult body composition.

## Chemistry

### 6. Avogadro's Number
**Question:** What is Avogadro's number (in sextillions)?
**Answer:** 602 sextillion (6.022 × 10²³)
**Source:** International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry (IUPAC)
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Notes:** Number of atoms in 12 grams of carbon-12.

### 7. Elements in Periodic Table
**Question:** How many confirmed elements are in the periodic table?
**Answer:** 118 elements
**Source:** IUPAC, Royal Society of Chemistry
**Difficulty:** Easy
**Notes:** As of 2023, with element 118 named Oganesson.

### 8. pH of Pure Water
**Question:** What is the pH of pure water at 25°C?
**Answer:** 7.0
**Source:** American Chemical Society
**Difficulty:** Easy
**Notes:** Neutral pH, but varies slightly with temperature.

### 9. Melting Point of Gold
**Question:** What is the melting point of gold in degrees Celsius?
**Answer:** 1,064°C
**Source:** National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)
**Difficulty:** Medium
**Notes:** Gold's high melting point contributes to its value.

### 10. Number of Molecules in Water Drop
**Question:** How many water molecules are in a typical drop of water (in quintillions)?
**Answer:** 1.67 quintillion molecules (1.67 × 10²¹)
**Source:** University of Illinois Physics Department
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Notes:** Based on 0.05mL drop volume.

## Biology & Life Sciences

### 11. Human DNA Base Pairs
**Question:** How many base pairs are in the human genome (in billions)?
**Answer:** 3.2 billion base pairs
**Source:** Human Genome Project, National Human Genome Research Institute
**Difficulty:** Medium-Hard
**Notes:** Varies slightly between individuals.

### 12. Heart Beats per Lifetime
**Question:** How many times does the average human heart beat in a lifetime (in billions)?
**Answer:** 2.5 billion beats
**Source:** American Heart Association, Harvard Medical School
**Difficulty:** Medium
**Notes:** Based on 60-80 bpm for 66-year average lifespan.

### 13. Number of Cells in Human Body
**Question:** How many cells are in an average human body (in trillions)?
**Answer:** 37.2 trillion cells
**Source:** Nature, "An estimation of the number of cells in the human body"
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Notes:** Recent 2013 study updated previous estimates.

### 14. Species on Earth
**Question:** How many species are estimated to exist on Earth (in millions)?
**Answer:** 8.7 million species
**Source:** PLoS Biology, "How Many Species Are There on Earth?"
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Notes:** Only 1.2 million have been documented.

### 15. Human Brain Neurons
**Question:** How many neurons are in the human brain (in billions)?
**Answer:** 86 billion neurons
**Source:** Brazilian Academy of Sciences, "The Human Brain in Numbers"
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Notes:** Updated from previous estimates of 100 billion.

## Earth Science & Environment

### 16. Earth's Age
**Question:** How old is the Earth in billions of years?
**Answer:** 4.54 billion years
**Source:** US Geological Survey, NASA
**Difficulty:** Medium
**Notes:** Determined through radiometric dating of meteorites.

### 17. Ocean Coverage
**Question:** What percentage of Earth's surface is covered by oceans?
**Answer:** 71%
**Source:** National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA)
**Difficulty:** Easy
**Notes:** Pacific Ocean alone covers 30% of Earth's surface.

### 18. Mount Everest Height
**Question:** What is the height of Mount Everest above sea level in meters?
**Answer:** 8,849 meters
**Source:** Nepal and China joint measurement (2020)
**Difficulty:** Easy-Medium
**Notes:** Official height as of 2020 measurement.

### 19. Earth's Core Temperature
**Question:** What is the temperature at Earth's inner core in degrees Celsius?
**Answer:** 5,700°C
**Source:** US Geological Survey, Nature Geoscience
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Notes:** Similar to Sun's surface temperature.

### 20. Amazon Rainforest Trees
**Question:** How many trees are in the Amazon rainforest (in billions)?
**Answer:** 390 billion trees
**Source:** Nature, "Mapping tree density at a global scale"
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Notes:** Represents 90 billion metric tons of carbon.

## Measurement & Quantities

### 21. Speed of Sound in Air
**Question:** What is the speed of sound in dry air at 20°C in meters per second?
**Answer:** 343 m/s
**Source:** NASA, Physics textbooks
**Difficulty:** Easy-Medium
**Notes:** Varies with temperature and humidity.

### 22. Earth's Rotation Speed
**Question:** How fast does Earth rotate at the equator in km/h?
**Answer:** 1,674 km/h
**Source:** NASA, Physics Factbook
**Difficulty:** Medium
**Notes:** Decreases with latitude; zero at poles.

### 23. Water on Earth
**Question:** What percentage of Earth's water is freshwater?
**Answer:** 2.5%
**Source:** US Geological Survey
**Difficulty:** Easy-Medium
**Notes:** Only 1.2% is surface freshwater; rest is groundwater.

### 24. Lightning Strikes per Second
**Question:** How many lightning strikes occur globally per second?
**Answer:** 44 lightning strikes per second
**Source:** NASA Lightning Imaging Sensor, Vaisala
**Difficulty:** Medium
**Notes:** Equals about 1.4 billion strikes per year.

### 25. Human Blood Volume
**Question:** How many liters of blood are in an average adult human body?
**Answer:** 4.5-5.5 liters
**Source:** American Red Cross, Medical textbooks
**Difficulty:** Easy
**Notes:** About 7-8% of body weight.
